# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People planning practical Indian meals around calorie targets, protein targets, dietary level, and food preferences. They are usually in a task mindset: enter targets quickly, generate a usable day, then adjust meals without losing the plan.

## Product Purpose

Meal Plan Calculator generates realistic Indian meal plans from calories, optional macro constraints, dietary level, and taste preferences. Success means the user gets a plan with practical serving increments, explicit target pass/fail feedback, and clear recovery paths when constraints cannot be satisfied.

## Brand Personality

Compact, warm, utilitarian. The product should feel calm and trustworthy, with enough editorial warmth to avoid a clinical dashboard feel, but never like a marketing page.

## Anti-references

Avoid copy-heavy onboarding, large hero sections, generic SaaS redesigns, dashboard chrome, bright gradients, glassmorphism, heavy shadows, and decorative imagery. Do not add explanatory panels above the primary calorie/protein fields or push the main inputs below the first mobile viewport.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the calculator first: input speed and result clarity outrank visual drama.
2. Preserve compact mobile hierarchy: calories, protein, diet, and Generate should stay immediately reachable.
3. Show only actionable guidance: recovery copy appears when a result fails, not as persistent onboarding.
4. Keep controls practical: quantities, locks, swaps, and meal tools should behave predictably and use familiar affordances.
5. Earn trust with exactness: realistic serving increments, explicit rejection reasons, and no fake successful plans.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Maintain visible keyboard focus, 44px minimum touch targets, sufficient contrast for body and muted text, native controls where possible, and reduced-motion-safe transitions. Nutrition output is estimate-based and should not imply medical certainty.
